About Melbourne Trains

Learn about the extensive rail network that connects the Greater Melbourne area

Australia's Largest Urban Rail Network

The Melbourne rail network is the backbone of public transport in Victoria, carrying over 240 million passenger trips annually. With its distinctive green and gold livery, the trains are an iconic part of Melbourne's identity.

The network spans over 372 kilometers of track, connecting the city center with suburbs as far as Pakenham, Cranbourne, Sunbury, and Werribee. The system operates from approximately 5:00 AM to midnight on most lines, with Night Network services on weekends.

All metropolitan trains are electric, powered by an overhead 1500V DC system, and operate on standard gauge tracks. The network is managed by Metro Trains Melbourne under contract to the Victorian Government.

Train Models

Explore the different train models operating across Melbourne's network

Waratah B Set

The Waratah trains are a modern electric multiple unit train operating on the Sydney Trains network. Known for their distinctive red front and advanced passenger amenities.

Tangara T Set

The Tangara is a type of electric multiple unit train that operates on the Sydney Trains network. Introduced in the late 1980s, these trains feature comfortable seating and reliable performance.

S Set (Low Poly)

The S Set trains were introduced in the 1970s and are among the oldest still in service on the Sydney Trains network. Known for their distinctive silver and orange livery.

Victorian A2

The Victorian Railways A2 class is a class of 4-6-0 steam locomotives built for the Victorian Railways between 1907 and 1915. These historic locomotives played a crucial role in Victoria's rail history.

Soviet Train

This high-poly Soviet-era train model showcases the distinctive design aesthetic of Eastern European railway engineering. These robust trains were known for their durability and functionality.

Alstom Citadis X05 Tram

Discover Melbourne's modern tram network featuring the advanced Alstom Citadis X05

Revolutionizing Urban Transport

The Alstom Citadis X05 is the latest generation of trams operating in Melbourne, part of the city's commitment to sustainable and efficient public transport. These low-floor trams provide exceptional accessibility and passenger comfort.

With a capacity of up to 210 passengers, the Citadis X05 features state-of-the-art technology including regenerative braking, air conditioning, real-time passenger information, and spacious interiors designed for Melbourne's diverse commuter needs.

The trams operate across Melbourne's extensive tram network, which is the largest in the world with over 250 kilometers of track and 1,763 tram stops. The network carries over 200 million passenger trips annually.

Key features include 100% low-floor design for easy boarding, energy-efficient LED lighting, advanced climate control systems, and quiet operation that reduces noise pollution in urban areas.

Historical Timeline

Key milestones in the development of Melbourne's rail network

1854

First Railway Line

Melbourne's first railway line opened between Flinders Street and Port Melbourne, making it one of the first cities in Australia with a railway system.

1919

Electrification Begins

The Melbourne rail network began its transition to electric trains, starting with the suburban lines to facilitate faster and more efficient services.

1981

Comeng Trains Introduced

The first Comeng trains entered service, eventually becoming the backbone of the Melbourne fleet for decades.

2009

Myki Implementation

The Myki smartcard ticketing system was introduced, replacing the previous paper-based Metcard system.

2020

High Capacity Metro Trains

The first High Capacity Metro Trains entered service, featuring modern amenities and increased passenger capacity.
